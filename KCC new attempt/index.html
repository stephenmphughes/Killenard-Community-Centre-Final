A Quick History of Killenard Community Centre

Explore the various facilities we offer to cater to all your needs.

1950’s

The Old School

Killenard in the 1950s was a quiet rural village in County Laois, Ireland, known for its close-knit community and agricultural heritage. Life revolved around farming, local markets, and the parish church, which was the heart of social and spiritual gatherings. Despite limited infrastructure, the village thrived on shared traditions, simple living, and a strong sense of unity among its residents.

1990’s

The Big Build

The Killenard Community Centre was built to serve as a vibrant hub for the local community. Its main hall, a spacious and versatile area, was designed for events, sports, and gatherings, accommodating various activities with ease.

2000’s

March 2024

In the 2000s, Killenard grew rapidly, marked by the construction of a modern school to meet rising educational needs and the Heritage Hotel, which boosted local tourism. The village balanced development with its community-focused charm, becoming a vibrant hub..

Present Day

September 2024

Today, the Killenard Community Centre is thriving, serving as a vital hub for activities and events. With strong community support, it continues to grow and has ambitious plans to expand its facilities and programs for future generations.
